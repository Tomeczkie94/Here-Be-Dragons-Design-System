Usage Rules

--parchment-100 covers ≥ 80% of all surface area. --blood and --gold reserved for attention elements. Never use --emerald and --sapphire together. For gold-colored text on parchment, use --gold-deep (5.4:1 contrast, WCAG AA) — never --gold or --gold-bright.

III

Texture & Atmosphere

The manuscript atmosphere is applied via two fixed pseudo-elements on body. No image assets — pure CSS radial gradients and an inline SVG turbulence filter.

body::before — Stains & Vignette

Three radial gradients: warm amber at top-left (15% 10%), deeper stain at bottom-right (85% 80%), and a dark-edged vignette at center. Creates the illusion of aged, handled parchment.

radial-gradient(
  ellipse 800px 600px at 15% 10%,
  rgba(168,141,90,0.18),
  transparent 60%
),
radial-gradient(
  ellipse at center,
  transparent 40%,
  rgba(58,40,23,0.25) 100%
)
body::after — Paper-Fibre Noise

Inline SVG feTurbulence fractalNoise at baseFrequency 0.85, 4 octaves, stitchTiles enabled. Opacity 0.28, mix-blend-mode: multiply. Purely decorative — pointer-events: none.

<feTurbulence
  type="fractalNoise"
  baseFrequency="0.85"
  numOctaves="4"
  stitchTiles="stitch"/>

Accessibility

Both pseudo-elements use pointer-events: none so they never intercept clicks or text selection. Content sits at z-index: 2 above both layers. Text contrast remains WCAG 2.2 AA compliant after all overlays render.

The Draconic Tongue

Language · Primordial · Rare

Draconic is one of the oldest languages in existence and has changed very little since the first dragons taught it to their dragonborn servants. It is the language of magic — every arcane formula derives its power from Draconic phonemes.

The alphabet is composed of sharp, angular runes called dracoglyphs. Each rune encodes not merely a phoneme but a concept — consonants carry elemental power, vowels carry intent and direction.

Sage Advice

A DC 15 Arcana check allows a character who does not know Draconic to understand the gist of a spoken phrase. The Linguist feat permits learning it as an additional language.
