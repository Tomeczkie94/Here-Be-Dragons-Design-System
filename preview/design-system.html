Usage Rules

--parchment-100 covers ≥ 80% of all surface area. --blood and --gold are reserved for elements demanding attention. Never use --emerald and --sapphire together on the same component. Never use --gold or --gold-bright as text color on parchment — use --gold-deep (5.4:1 contrast ratio, WCAG AA compliant) instead.

Typography

The type foundation pairs five token-driven families — Tiamat Condensed SC for display, Cinzel for accent headings, IM Fell English for serif body and flavour text, Roboto for UI labels, and JetBrains Mono for code. Apply the tag-agnostic .hbd-text-* utilities on any element to render text at the correct scale, line-height, and weight.

III

Texture & Atmosphere

The manuscript atmosphere is applied via two fixed pseudo-elements on body. No image assets — pure CSS radial gradients and an inline SVG turbulence filter.

body::before — Stains & Vignette

Three radial gradients: warm amber at top-left (15% 10%), deeper stain at bottom-right (85% 80%), and a dark-edged vignette at center. Creates the illusion of aged, handled parchment.

radial-gradient(
  ellipse 800px 600px at 15% 10%,
  rgba(168,141,90,0.18),
  transparent 60%
),
radial-gradient(
  ellipse at center,
  transparent 40%,
  rgba(58,40,23,0.25) 100%
)
body::after — Paper-Fibre Noise

Inline SVG feTurbulence fractalNoise with baseFrequency 0.85 and 4 octaves, stitchTiles enabled. Opacity 0.28, mix-blend-mode: multiply. Purely decorative — pointer-events: none, z-index: 1.

<feTurbulence
  type="fractalNoise"
  baseFrequency="0.85"
  numOctaves="4"
  stitchTiles="stitch"/>

Accessibility

Both pseudo-elements use pointer-events: none so they never intercept clicks or text selection. Content sits at z-index: 2, above both texture layers. Text contrast remains WCAG 2.2 AA compliant after all overlays render.

The Draconic Tongue

Language · Primordial · Rare

Draconic is one of the oldest languages in existence and has changed very little since the first dragons taught it to their dragonborn servants. It is the language of magic — every arcane formula derives its power from Draconic phonemes.

The alphabet is composed of sharp, angular runes called dracoglyphs. Each rune encodes not merely a phoneme but a concept — consonants carry elemental power, vowels carry intent and direction.

Sage Advice

A successful DC 15 Arcana check allows a character who does not know Draconic to understand the gist of a spoken phrase. The Linguist feat permits learning it as an additional language.

          Accessibility

          ARIA Pattern
          • Trigger: role="button" aria-haspopup="dialog" aria-expanded
          • Panel: role="dialog" aria-label aria-modal="false"
          • Columns: role="listbox" with unique aria-label
          • Rows: role="option" aria-selected
          • Header: aria-hidden="true" — decorative only
          Keyboard & Focus
          • Esc — close panel, return focus to trigger
          • Enter / Space — select focused option
          • Click outside — closes panel
          • Focus ring: 2px solid --gold-deep (light) / --parchment-100 (Confirm button)
          • No scrollIntoView() — uses scrollTop only
